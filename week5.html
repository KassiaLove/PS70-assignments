3D Modeling and Printing

For the 3D printing thing this week, I didn't do anything too fancy. To gpt use to the 3D printers in the lab, I followed the maker's coin tutorial provided: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tGtNLpYSXOU. When starting out with the 3D printing, the major problem I was running into was the PLA not sticking to the hot plate. I Googled around and found out that maybe the build plate just needs to be cleaned again with isoproply. Trying this, and learning how to unload and load the filaments eventually worked to have the print work. One reason why is was having a lack of adhesion was that something would come out of the nozzle wrong and would leave an uneven layer so when the nozzle came back around to lay down another layer, it would get caught on the part that is uneven and the pull it off of the build plate. The second reason was that there was probably a build up of oil on the build plate to cleaning it with isoproply did the trick.

The basic mechanism for make the maker coin in Fusion 360 was drawing a base circle, then make the teeth out of 2 circles, and placing a letter on the front of the coin. One coin by itself took like 20 mins to print.

Next I wanted to make something that can be used for practical use. So I decided to make a small pencil/pen holder. What makes it cool is that it is a hexagon shape that swirls from the bottom up. Through making this I learned about the offset plane, loft, and shell settings. For the offset plane setting, you can make a plane a x distance away from an object. For loft, it will connect the points of 2 shapes. And for shell, it will hollow your item to your liking. These settings made it really easy to making this pencil/pen holder, and I can easily make more in the future.

Pros of 3D printing:

  • Can make more complex models that you can not laser cut or use shop tools to make
  • Doesn't have to be flat, as it sorta has to be for laser cutting
  • Many different types of 3d printing
  • Variety of materials

Cons of 3D printing:

  • Takes time to print
  • If things aren't set up correct, the print will fail
  • Not really eco-friendly
